About me

About me

End of summer presentation at General AtomicsHi! I’m Riley, a mechanical engineer with a passion for hands-on problem solving. I’ve gained significant experience in manufacturing, particularly in the medical device and aerospace industries, where precision and innovation are key. My professional background has given me the opportunity to work on cutting-edge projects, ensuring that the products I help develop meet the highest standards of quality and functionality.

Outside of work, I love diving into personal projects that let me get my hands dirty—whether it’s woodworking, tinkering with electronics, or experimenting with my home server. One of my favorite side projects was setting up a self-hosted media server using Jellyfin, turning my server into a personal streaming platform that acts like my own version of Netflix. With a strong foundation in engineering and a drive to continually learn and create, I’m always looking for new challenges, both in the professional sphere and at home in my workshop.

Skills

Skill Description
SolidWorks Used at Rev1 Engineering as well as throughout Mech.E. major. Created 25+ fixtures for company and gearbox model for design project.
Creo / Windchill Used at General Atomics. Created portable aileron display assembly.
MatLab Programming Created MatLab program to analyze INSTRON test of braided catheters at Rev1. Used MatLab throughout Mech.E. degree for various classes and projects published on GitHub.

Collaboration Throughout both internships I had weekly meetings with engineering teams to ensure that the projects stayed on track. I've also collaborated with technicians and assemblers to gather feedback on fixture design.
